# Ayla — Marketing Website Spec

This is the brief for the agent building **aylainsights.com** (or whichever domain
lands). It's self-contained — you don't need to read the rest of the repo to
do this job. If you want extra context for tone, see `PROJECT_SPEC.md` (what
the product does) and `CLAUDE.md` (how seriously we take PHI/HIPAA — this
matters for your messaging).

> **Read the "Critical guardrails" section first.** It contains the only
> non-negotiable rules — most of them are about what you cannot say.

---

## 1. What we sell, who we sell to

**Product (one sentence):** Ayla is an AI copilot for dental
practices that finds the money hiding in their charts and helps the team book
it — unscheduled treatment, schedule gaps, lapsed recall.

**Company:** Ayla Insights.

**Primary audience:** practice owners and office managers at independent and
small-group dental practices (1–5 locations). They use **Dentrix** today.
They are running their practice, not shopping for AI tools. They feel:
overwhelmed at the front desk, sure there's money slipping through the
cracks, skeptical of dental-tech vendors who oversold them last time.

**Secondary audience:** front-desk staff who'll be the daily users (need to
trust that the tool helps, doesn't replace them), and any investors/partners
who land on the site.

**What we're not:** a patient-facing app, a marketing automation tool, a
generic chatbot, or a replacement for Dentrix.

## 2. Positioning

**The hook (use variants of this throughout):**
> The average independent practice has **$100,000+ of unscheduled treatment**
> sitting in patient charts. Ayla finds it, surfaces it, and helps your
> team book it.

**Why we win:**
- **Numbers-forward, not vibes-forward.** Ayla shows actual dollar amounts —
  unscheduled treatment, fillable hours this week, overdue recall — not
  vague "insights."
- **Works with Dentrix.** No rip-and-replace. Ayla reads from the PMS the
  practice already runs.
- **Front desk stays in control.** Ayla can *propose* bookings; nothing
  happens until the staff member taps Confirm. This is the trust story —
  lead with it whenever you talk about AI safety.
- **Built for HIPAA from day one.** (See guardrails — phrase carefully.)

**What we are not selling on:** model brand-names ("powered by Claude"),
prompt-engineering wizardry, agentic AI buzz. The audience does not care.
They care about money found and time saved.

## 3. Critical guardrails — read before writing copy

These are non-negotiable. Most relate to legal/compliance risk.

### 3.1 Do not claim "HIPAA compliant"

HIPAA compliance is a *legal status*, not a feature, and we don't have it
yet (no BAAs executed, no risk analysis filed). Overclaiming this is real
liability. Use these alternative phrasings instead:

| ❌ Do not write | ✅ Write instead |
|---|---|
| "HIPAA compliant" | "Built for HIPAA" / "HIPAA-ready architecture" |
| "Fully secure" | "Designed around the HIPAA technical safeguards" |
| "Bank-grade encryption" | (describe specifics — TLS, encryption at rest, audit log) |
| "Your data is safe" | (describe what specifically — tenant isolation, audit trail, human-in-the-loop) |

If you describe a control we're still building (TLS termination, KMS, MFA),
phrase it as **what Ayla is built to do** or **the architecture supports**,
not "we have." When in doubt, ask the user.

### 3.2 No patient names, photos, or stories — real *or* synthetic-looking

Even synthetic patient data on a marketing page reads as if it could be
real. Dashboards in screenshots are fine, but **always with an obvious
"Demo data" watermark** in the image. No first-and-last name pairings, no
patient testimonials (we have none yet), no before/after stories.

### 3.3 No claims about competitors

Don't name Yapi, Weave, Modento, Lighthouse 360, Pearl, or any other dental
vendor. Don't write "unlike [X]." Position against the *status quo*
("spreadsheets and the report module"), not other products.

### 3.4 No medical/clinical claims

Ayla is a *front-office* tool. It does not diagnose, treat, or recommend
clinical care. If copy implies otherwise, rewrite it.

### 3.5 ROI numbers — be specific or be silent

If a page shows a number ($100K, $150K, "30% lift", etc.), it must either be
clearly labeled "**typical** for an independent practice" or "**estimated**"
or come with a footnote. Never invent a customer-attributed number ("Bright
Smile Dental saved $X"). The dashboard's headline numbers ($145K unscheduled,
$192K total opportunity, 117 overdue recalls) come from a seeded *demo*
practice — they are realistic-but-synthetic and can be cited as **"the kind
of opportunity Ayla finds in a typical practice."**

## 4. Goals & success criteria

**Primary goal:** a qualified practice owner or office manager books a
discovery call.

**Primary CTA (everywhere):** "Book a discovery call" (calendar embed).

**Secondary CTA:** "Join the waitlist" (email capture only). Use this for
visitors not ready to book.

**Out of scope for v1:** self-serve sign-up, free trial, in-product purchase.

**Measurable success (for the agent to instrument):**
- Lighthouse: Performance ≥95, Accessibility ≥95, Best Practices ≥95,
  SEO ≥95 on mobile *and* desktop.
- TTI < 2.5s on a Moto G4 / Slow 4G profile.
- Discovery-call form submission, waitlist email submission, demo-video play,
  and pricing-page view all instrumented as events.

## 6. Page-by-page content blueprint

For each page below, draft headlines and proof points are starting points,
not contracts. Rewrite for flow — keep substance and structure.

### 6.1 Home (`/`)

**Hero (above the fold):**
- Eyebrow: *AI for the dental front office*
- Headline: **Find the revenue hiding in your practice.**
- Subhead: *The average independent practice has over $100,000 of
  unscheduled treatment sitting in patient charts. Ayla shows your team
  exactly where the opportunity is — and helps them book it.*
- Primary CTA: **Book a discovery call**
- Secondary CTA: *Join the waitlist*
- Visual: a clean screenshot of the Ayla dashboard, with the "Money on the
  table" cards prominent. Watermark: "Demo data."

**Below the fold (top to bottom):**

1. **The three opportunity buckets** (3-card row, each with a dashboard
   crop):
   - **Unscheduled treatment.** Diagnosed cases that never got booked,
     ranked by dollars and recency.
   - **Fillable hours this week.** Open chair time, with estimated
     production each gap could recover.
   - **Lapsed recall.** Hygiene patients past due, ranked by months
     overdue × historical show-rate.

2. **How it works** (3-step horizontal):
   - **Connect.** Ayla reads from Dentrix — no rip-and-replace.
   - **Discover.** The dashboard shows where the money is, every morning.
   - **Act.** Ask Ayla in chat — *"Fill tomorrow's openings,"* *"Find
     unscheduled crowns over $1,000."* Your team confirms every booking.

3. **The trust story** (one short panel):
   - Headline: *Ayla suggests. Your team decides.*
   - Body: Every booking and cancellation goes through a confirmation card.
     Nothing happens until a staff member taps Confirm. Every action is
     audited.

4. **Built for HIPAA** (security panel, leads to `/security`):
   - One paragraph describing the posture (tenant isolation, audit trail,
     minimum-necessary data sent to AI, BAA-ready). End with a "See our
     security posture" link.

5. **Final CTA panel:** *Book a 20-minute discovery call. We'll show you
   what Ayla would find in a practice like yours.*

### 6.2 Product (`/product`)

Deeper version of "How it works." Sections:
- **The dashboard** (with a larger annotated screenshot — Demo data
  watermark). Walk through what each card means and why it matters.
- **Ask Ayla** — the chat console. Show 3–4 example questions and the
  kinds of answers the user gets. Emphasize that **patient names are
  masked when sent to the AI** ("First L.") — describe this as a deliberate
  privacy architecture, not a workaround.
- **The booking flow** — proposal → confirmation card → booked. Walk it
  step by step. Reinforce: the AI cannot book without staff confirmation.
- **The audit trail** — every action logged, every read of patient data
  logged, by user and timestamp.
- **What Ayla doesn't do** (a short, honest list — builds trust):
  - Doesn't send SMS/email to patients on its own (yet — display-only
    outreach scripts for staff to copy)
  - Doesn't give clinical advice
  - Doesn't replace front-desk staff

### 6.3 Security (`/security`)

This is the page that converts office managers and operations leads. Be
specific, not waffly.

Suggested sections:
- **The posture** — Ayla is built around the HIPAA technical safeguards.
  We expect to sign a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with every
  customer before any real patient data flows.
- **What we built in from day one** (bulleted list — the language below
  matches what's actually shipped in the codebase; do not embellish):
  - **Tenant isolation by construction.** Each practice's data is reached
    through a tenant-bound data adapter; cross-tenant reads are
    structurally impossible.
  - **Human-in-the-loop writes.** The AI can propose appointments and
    cancellations; nothing executes until a staff member confirms.
  - **The AI never touches the database.** It can only call a whitelisted
    set of validated tools.
  - **Minimum-necessary to AI.** Patient names are masked, and phone,
    email, DOB, and outreach scripts never reach the language model.
  - **PHI-scrubbed logs.** Patient data cannot land in server logs.
  - **Tamper-evident audit trail.** Every action and every read of patient
    data is logged, tenant-scoped, and retained.
- **What's coming before launch** (be honest — this signals seriousness):
  - TLS end-to-end and encryption at rest
  - SSO + MFA, RBAC, automatic session expiry
  - Executed BAAs with every business associate (Anthropic, our cloud
    host, etc.)
  - Documented risk analysis, written policies, designated Security Officer
  - Independent HIPAA gap assessment before any real PHI flows
- **Contact:** a "Talk to our security team" mailto (use `security@ayla...`
  — flag the address as TBD).

### 6.4 Pricing (`/pricing`)

Pre-launch — no public pricing yet. Keep it short and honest:

- Headline: *Pricing built for independent practices.*
- One short paragraph: we're still partnering directly with our launch
  customers to set pricing that pays for itself in the first month.
- Two buttons: **Book a discovery call** and **Join the waitlist**.

> **TBD:** when pricing is set, this page becomes a tiered/usage table.
> Out of scope for v1.

### 6.5 About (`/about`)

- Mission: a paragraph on why Ayla exists. Theme: dental practices leave
  hundreds of thousands of dollars unbilled and untreated every year — not
  because they're bad at their jobs, but because the systems they use don't
  surface it. We built Ayla to fix that.
- **TBD: founder bio.** Placeholder block — the user will fill in name,
  background, photo, and a 1–2 paragraph story.
- (Optional, when team grows: a small team grid. Skip for v1.)

### 6.6 Book (`/book`)

A focused single-purpose page: short reassurance ("20 minutes. We'll show
you what Ayla would find in a practice your size. No pitch deck.") plus an
embedded calendar widget (Cal.com or Calendly — see Section 9).

### 6.7 Waitlist (`/waitlist`)

Minimal email-capture form. Required fields: name, work email, practice
name. Optional: PMS in use (defaults to Dentrix), # locations, # providers.
Confirmation page or inline confirmation message.

### 6.8 Legal (`/legal/privacy`, `/legal/terms`)

Use boilerplate templates appropriate for a B2B SaaS handling marketing-site
visitor data only (no PHI flows through the website itself). Mark
prominently: **"Last updated [DATE]. Reviewed by counsel: pending."** —
remove the "pending" line once a lawyer signs off.

### 6.9 Blog (`/blog`)

Scaffold the IA only. No posts at launch. The system should support MDX
posts when the user is ready to write.

## 7. Voice & tone

| Be this | Not this |
|---|---|
| Confident, calm, specific | Hype-y ("revolutionary AI") |
| Numbers-forward | Vibes-forward ("delightful experiences") |
| Practitioner-first (front-desk language) | Engineer-first ("agentic LLM pipeline") |
| Honest about limits ("display-only outreach for now") | Vague hand-waving |
| Sentences | Walls of em-dashes |

**Reference voice:** Stripe.com home page (calm, specific, design-led). Not
Notion.so (cute), not OpenAI.com (cosmic).

**Words to use:** copilot, front office, opportunity, recover, find, surface,
confirm, audit, practice (always "practice," never "clinic" or "office").

**Words to avoid:** revolutionize, unleash, supercharge, "powered by AI,"
"intelligent," "smart" (as adjective), "amazing," "delightful," "patients
love it" (we have no patient users), 🎉/🚀 emoji.

## 8. Visual identity

The product uses a teal/600 brand color. Carry it. The site should feel
**clean, dental-SaaS-professional, design-led, not flashy**.

### Tokens (starting set — refine if you have a strong opinion)

```css

--brand-100: #ccfbf1;
--brand-500: #14b8a6;

--brand-700: #0f766e;
--brand-900: #134e4a;

--ink-900:   #0f172a;  /* headings */
--ink-700:   #334155;  /* body */
--ink-500:   #64748b;  /* secondary text */

--bg:        #ffffff;
--bg-soft:   #f8fafc;
--accent:    #f59e0b;  /* sparingly — for highlight stats only */
```

### Typography

- Sans for everything; default to `Inter` or a comparable open-source
  sans-serif via `fonts.bunny.net` or `fontsource` (avoid Google Fonts'
  privacy quirks — see §11).
- Headings: tighter tracking, –1% to –2%.
- Body: comfortable line-height (1.6+), generous max-width (~640px for
  reading sections).

### Look & feel

- Light, lots of white space.
- Real product screenshots > stock photos. **Always watermark screenshots
  with "Demo data."**
- Limited animation: subtle on-scroll fades only. No parallax, no carousels.
- One illustration set or none — if you go custom, get the user to approve
  style before producing the set.

### Logo

> **TBD:** the user has not provided a finalized logo. Build the site with a
> simple wordmark ("Ayla") in `--brand-700` and the small "A" mark from the
> app (rounded square, gradient `--brand-500 → --brand-700`, white "A"). Make
> swapping a real logo a one-file change.

## 9. Functional requirements

- **Calendar booking** (`/book`): embed Cal.com (preferred — open source,
  no surveillance) or Calendly. **TBD:** the user's specific calendar link.
- **Waitlist + discovery-call form**: send to a serverless endpoint. Store
  submissions in Airtable, Notion, or a simple Postgres table. **Email the
  user on submit.** Recommend Resend for transactional email. Do not roll
  your own SMTP.
- **Newsletter signup** (optional, post-launch): hook into the same backend
  as waitlist; mark as a `kind: 'newsletter'` field.
- **404 / 500 pages** that link back home.
- **Sitemap + robots.txt + Open Graph + JSON-LD** (see §10).

## 10. SEO, performance, accessibility

### SEO

- One `<h1>` per page; semantic headings.
- Title tags ≤ 60 chars; meta descriptions ≤ 155 chars; each page
  individually written.
- Open Graph + Twitter Card on every page with a 1200×630 social card. The
  home and product pages get a custom designed card; other pages can use a
  template card.
- `sitemap.xml` and `robots.txt` autogenerated.
- JSON-LD structured data on home and product: `SoftwareApplication` schema
  with `applicationCategory: "BusinessApplication"`.

### Performance

- Lighthouse mobile + desktop scores ≥95 across the board.
- Largest Contentful Paint < 1.5s on broadband, < 2.5s on Slow 4G.
- No more than 100KB of JS on the home page (target). Static-first.
- Self-host fonts (subset to Latin if possible). Avoid Google Fonts CDN.
- Image budget: WebP or AVIF only, sized for the viewport, served via
  `next/image` or `astro:assets`.

### Accessibility

- WCAG 2.1 AA target.
- Keyboard-navigable everywhere; visible focus states (don't reset).
- All buttons are `<button>`, all links are `<a>`, no `<div onclick>`.
- Color-contrast ratio ≥4.5:1 for body, ≥3:1 for large text.
- Forms with `<label>` and inline error messages.
- Screen-reader-only headings for visual sections that lack one.

## 11. Privacy & analytics

The audience is healthcare-adjacent. Be conservative.

- **Analytics:** Plausible, Fathom, or Vercel Analytics. **No Google
  Analytics.** No third-party cookies. No cookie banner needed if we don't
  set tracking cookies.
- **Fonts:** self-host or use `fonts.bunny.net` — do not load from
  fonts.googleapis.com (data-protection concerns).
- **Embeds:** if Calendly/Cal.com is embedded, use the version that loads
  on user click ("Click to load calendar"), not auto-load. Same for any
  video.
- **Forms:** TLS only. Store nothing in cookies that doesn't need to be.

## 12. Tech stack — recommendation

You have judgment here. Defaults the user will be happy with:

- **Framework:** Astro (preferred — content-site native, ships minimal JS)
  or Next.js App Router. Either is fine; avoid SPA frameworks (no
  marketing-site CSR).
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS. Match the design tokens in §8.
- **Hosting:** Vercel or Netlify. Whichever the framework you pick prefers.
- **Forms backend:** a Vercel/Netlify function + Resend for email + a
  managed table (Airtable to start; Supabase if you want a queryable DB).
- **Domain:** **TBD.** Build the site so DNS + custom domain is a config
  change, not a code change.
- **Repo:** new repo, separate from the product. **Do not commit secrets**
  (Resend API key, Cal.com tokens) — use the host's env-var system.

## 14. Acceptance criteria

The site is ready to ship when:

1. All pages in §5 are live, content matches §6, voice matches §7.
2. Lighthouse ≥95 across Performance, Accessibility, Best Practices, SEO
   on **both** mobile and desktop, for the home page and `/product` at
   minimum.
3. Discovery-call form, waitlist form, and analytics events all work
   end-to-end (form submissions arrive in the chosen backend; the user
   receives an email per submission).
4. No claim of "HIPAA compliant" appears anywhere on the site.
5. Every patient-facing screenshot carries a visible "Demo data"
   watermark.
6. The site renders correctly with **JS disabled** for the home, product,
   and security pages (forms and calendar embeds can require JS).
7. A `404` page exists; broken links from the nav and footer return 0.
8. The repo's README contains: how to run locally, how to deploy, how to
   change content, and where the env vars live.
