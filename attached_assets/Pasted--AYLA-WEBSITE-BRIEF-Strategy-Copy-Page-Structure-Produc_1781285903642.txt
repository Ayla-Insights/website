# AYLA WEBSITE BRIEF — Strategy, Copy & Page Structure

## Product
Ayla is an AI front-office agent for dental practices. It integrates with the
Dentrix practice management system and gives practices two things:
1. A staff chat console — front desk asks plain-English questions, Ayla
   surfaces opportunities and takes scheduling actions.
2. A patient-facing web chat — 24/7 self-scheduling that writes into Dentrix.

## The core strategic insight (drives ALL copy decisions)
Discovery interviews revealed that the dentist's #1 wish wasn't better
reporting — it was staff alignment toward growth. Practices know their
numbers are bad. They don't have the tools to act on them OR to get their
teams rowing in the same direction.

This means Ayla's positioning is NOT "analytics platform." It is:
> The operating system for a growing dental practice.

Every headline, feature description, and CTA should connect to one of three
outcomes: fill the schedule, recover lost production, align the team around
growth. Numbers and dollar signs belong in the copy — vague benefit language
does not.

---

## Brand voice
- Confident but not arrogant. Practitioners are smart; don't oversell.
- Specific and numerical. "6 figures of unscheduled treatment" beats
  "revenue opportunities."
- Practitioner-empathetic. Acknowledge the reality: dentists didn't go to
  school to manage software or coach reluctant staff.
- Never clinical. Ayla does not give clinical advice and the website should
  never imply it does.
- Trust-forward. Security and HIPAA compliance are features, not footnotes.

### The problem section
Headline: "Dental practices lose money the same way, every time."

Three-column layout (icon + short copy each):

1. Empty chairs
   A cancelled appointment with no backfill is pure lost revenue. Most
   offices have no fast way to find a replacement.

2. Unscheduled treatment
   The average practice has $100,000+ in diagnosed-but-unscheduled
   treatment plans. Nobody has time to chase them.

3. Staff flying blind
   Your team can't grow what they can't see. Without clear goals and
   real-time pacing, growth stays a conversation instead of becoming
   a result.

---

### How it works (3 steps — keep it visual)
1. Connect to Dentrix
   Ayla integrates directly with your Dentrix system — no manual data
   entry, no spreadsheets. Setup takes minutes.

2. See your opportunities instantly
   Your dashboard opens on the numbers that matter: unscheduled treatment,
   schedule gaps, recall patients, goal pacing. In real dollars.

3. Ask Ayla to act
   Type a question — "Who can fill tomorrow's 2pm?" or "Show me
   unscheduled crowns over $1,000" — and Ayla finds the answer and
   proposes the action. You approve it. Done.

### Feature sections (individual scrolling sections or tabs)

FEATURE 1: Fill the schedule
Headline: "A cancellation isn't a lost hour — unless you let it be."
Copy: Ayla detects open slots and instantly identifies the best patients
to fill them, ranked by treatment need, recall status, and how long
they've been waiting. One click to reach out. One click to confirm.
The chair stays productive.

FEATURE 2: Recover unscheduled treatment
Headline: "Your most valuable patients are already in your system."
Copy: Ayla ranks every unscheduled treatment plan by dollar value and
time since diagnosis — so your team knows exactly who to call, what
to say, and what it's worth. Stop leaving five-figure cases sitting
in a report nobody reads.
Callout stat: "The average Ayla practice has $140,000 in unscheduled
treatment. Most of it is crowns, implants, and high-value elective work."

FEATURE 3: Align your team around growth
Headline: "Growth is a team sport. Give your team a scoreboard."
Copy: Ayla tracks production by provider, collections by front-office
activity, and hygiene productivity by hygienist — and shows everyone
their pacing toward monthly goals. Not to micromanage. To motivate.
When the team can see the gap, they can close it.
Quote (from discovery): "I need my staff to be as invested in growth
as I am." — General dentist, 20-year practice owner

FEATURE 4: Know which insurance plans are worth keeping
Headline: "Not all insurance is good insurance."
Copy: Ayla breaks down your production and collections by carrier,
calculates your effective reimbursement rate, and flags the plans
where write-offs are quietly killing your margin. Make the
participation decision with data, not gut feel.

FEATURE 5: 24/7 patient self-scheduling
Headline: "Patients book when you're not there. Now you capture them."
Copy: An embeddable web chat widget lets patients book, reschedule,
and confirm appointments any time — directly into your Dentrix
schedule, following your real scheduling rules. No double-booking.
No phone tag. No missed opportunities after hours.

### Security & compliance section
Headline: "Built for HIPAA from the ground up. Not bolted on after."
Copy: Ayla was designed by an IAM and cybersecurity architect with
enterprise defense-industry experience. Your patient data stays yours.
Encrypted in transit and at rest. Role-based access controls. Full
audit trail of every action. We sign a Business Associate Agreement
with every practice. Compliance isn't a checkbox for us — it's the
architecture.
Trust signals: HIPAA compliant badge, BAA included, SOC 2 (in progress),
role-based access, end-to-end encryption.

## Design direction notes for the web agent
- Clean, clinical-adjacent aesthetic. Think: modern health-tech SaaS.
  White backgrounds, generous whitespace, one strong accent color
  (suggest teal or a dental blue — founder to approve).
- Dashboard screenshot/animation is the most important visual asset.
  The numbers in that screenshot must look real and specific.
- Mobile-first. Practice managers check things on their phones.
- Trust signals (HIPAA, BAA, encryption) should appear near any data
  or integration mention — not just in a dedicated section.
- No stock photos of smiling dentists. Use product UI, data
  visualizations, and clean iconography instead.
- The hero needs a number. Whatever the headline, a specific dollar
  figure ($140,000, $148,000) should be visible above the fold.
